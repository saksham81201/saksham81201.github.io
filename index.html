About Me

I’m Saksham Verma, a passionate data and AI enthusiast currently pursuing my Master’s in Information Systems Technology at The George Washington University, along with a Graduate Certificate in Artificial Intelligence. My academic journey is driven by a strong foundation in computer engineering and a deep interest in building intelligent systems that bridge the gap between technology and business value.

I specialize in designing data-centric solutions that help organizations make smarter, faster decisions. My expertise lies in applying statistical analysis, machine learning, and system design to real-world problems—ranging from optimizing inventory for major retailers to building integrated platforms that streamline the job application process. I’ve gained practical experience through internships and academic projects that involved developing recommendation systems, analyzing crime and mobility data, and improving resume evaluation using ATS-aligned models.

Beyond technical skills, I’ve taken leadership roles as a Graduate Teaching Assistant, student mentor, and captain of the GWU Cricket Team, honing my ability to collaborate, communicate, and execute in fast-paced environments. I’m driven by curiosity, committed to continuous learning, and always eager to explore the next big problem where data, design, and decision-making intersect.

Whether it’s building scalable platforms, digging into complex datasets, or leading cross-functional teams, I bring a blend of analytical rigor and practical experience to the table—and I’m excited to apply it in building impactful, human-centered solutions.
